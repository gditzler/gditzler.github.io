Research Overview

My area of research falls under the umbrella of pattern recognition and and machine learning. My primary area of research is incremental and online learning under the assumption of concept drift. Concept drift is the problem of learning from time-series data when the training and testing distributions are changing with time. I have worked with several multiple classifier based-systems to handle conept drift with: (a) unbalanced data, and (b) transductive/semi-supervised learning.

I have also been involved with feature selection methods for metagenomic data. In particular, I have worked on studies of information theoretic methods applied to high-dimensional metagenomic OTU abundance data. Furthermore, I have worked on the development of wrapper based feature selection methods that provide inference of the number of relevant features in a data set.
